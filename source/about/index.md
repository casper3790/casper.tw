# About

## Intro

I am **Casper**, real name **Hong Xin-Fu**.

I am an engineering student in Taiwan learning **Cybersecurity**, **Web development**, **Robotics**, **Embedded systems**, and **Medical Cybersecurity**. I am not trying to make this site look like a finished corporate resume. It is closer to a public notebook from someone who is still building, testing, failing, debugging, and improving.

## What I Build

I like projects that become real systems, not only concepts on slides. My current work connects web platforms, security thinking, robotics experiments, embedded devices, and healthcare-related security questions.

Some topics I keep returning to:

- Cybersecurity and CTF practice
- Web systems with FastAPI and frontend workflows
- ROS2, Raspberry Pi, ESP32, and Robotics prototypes
- Medical Cybersecurity and data validation problems
- Tools that help a team organize technical work

## How I Learn

I learn by building. Competitions, workshops, failed experiments, debugging sessions, and project notes all become part of the record.

When something breaks, I try to write down what happened, what I misunderstood, and what I changed. That process matters to me because it turns scattered effort into something I can review later.

## Why This Site Exists

This website is my **public engineering notebook**.

I use it to practice **Build in Public**, organize projects and Labs, and share the road as it actually looks. Some pages are polished. Some are still notes. The point is to keep moving and keep the learning visible.
