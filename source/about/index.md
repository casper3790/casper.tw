  <p>Every journey starts somewhere.</p>

<section class="about-v1-chapter">
  <p class="about-v1-chapter-number">Chapter 1</p>
  <h2>The Beginning</h2>
  <p>I did not begin with a clear map.</p>
  <p>At first, technology was simply a place where curiosity had somewhere to go. I liked the feeling of opening something unfamiliar, breaking it into smaller pieces, and slowly understanding why it worked.</p>
  <p>That was the beginning of Casper: not a finished identity, but a quiet record of learning how to look more carefully.</p>
</section>

<section class="about-v1-chapter">
  <p class="about-v1-chapter-number">Chapter 2</p>
  <h2>A Choice</h2>
  <p>There was a point where I had to choose how I wanted to learn.</p>
  <p>I could wait until I felt ready, or I could start before everything made sense. I chose the second path: build a little, fail a little, write down what happened, and return with more patience.</p>
  <p>That choice changed how I saw progress. It became less about proving I was good enough, and more about staying honest with the process.</p>
</section>

<section class="about-v1-chapter">
  <p class="about-v1-chapter-number">Chapter 3</p>
  <h2>Not Alone</h2>
  <p>For a long time, learning felt personal. It was my confusion, my notes, my small victories, and my mistakes.</p>
  <p>But the more I wrote, the more I realized that a record can become useful to someone else. A note kept for myself might become a starting point for another person.</p>
  <p>That is why this site is public. It is not here to show that I already know everything. It is here because the process itself may be worth leaving behind.</p>
</section>

<section class="about-v1-chapter">
  <p class="about-v1-chapter-number">Chapter 4</p>
  <h2>Polaris</h2>
  <p>My Polaris is not a job title, a certificate, or a perfect plan.</p>
  <p>It is the direction that keeps pulling me back to learning, building, and paying attention to systems that affect real people.</p>
  <p>When I feel uncertain, I return to one question: what is worth understanding more deeply, even if it takes time?</p>
</section>

<section class="about-v1-chapter">
  <p class="about-v1-chapter-number">Chapter 5</p>
  <h2>Today</h2>
  <p>Today, Casper is still in progress.</p>
  <p>I am still learning how to ask better questions, how to build with more care, and how to write in a way that makes the path visible instead of hiding the unfinished parts.</p>
  <p>This page is not a summary of everything I can do. It is a snapshot of someone still moving forward.</p>
</section>

<section class="about-v1-chapter">
  <p class="about-v1-chapter-number">Chapter 6</p>
  <h2>What I Hope to Leave Behind</h2>
  <p>I hope to leave more than finished projects.</p>
  <p>I hope to leave traces of how things were learned: the wrong turns, the questions, the rebuilding, and the small moments where something finally became clear.</p>
  <p>If this site helps even one person feel less alone at the beginning, then it has already done something meaningful.</p>
</section>

<section class="about-v1-final-page">
  <p>For you, reading this page.</p>
  <p>If you still do not know what fits you,</p>
  <p>that is okay.</p>

  <p>Do not rush to compare yourself with others.</p>
  <p>First, find one thing</p>
  <p>that you are willing to keep doing.</p>
  <p>Leave the rest</p>
  <p>to time.</p>
