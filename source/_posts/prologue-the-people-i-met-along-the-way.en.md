# Prologue: Keeping the People and Stories That Walked With Me

If someone asked me:

> Why did you start writing a blog?

Honestly, at first I did not know how to answer.

Was it to record technical notes?

Was it to share Cybersecurity knowledge?

Was it to give myself another portfolio piece for future school applications or job searches?

All of those reasons were true.

But later I realized that the real reason was not exactly those things.

It was because I had started to fear forgetting.

---

Over the past few years, I have participated in many activities.

Some were security competitions.

Some were conferences.

Some were project competitions.

Some were robotics competitions.

Some were projects I joined simply because I was passionate about them.

I met many people.

Teachers.

Seniors.

Junior students.

Teammates.

Partners.

And people who once worked hard beside me.

Some of them walked with me for a long time.

Some were brief encounters at a single event.

But all of them left traces in my life.

---

I still remember how nervous I was the first time I joined a competition.

I still remember the excitement of receiving an award on stage for the first time.

I still remember the first time failure made me begin to doubt myself.

I also remember the people who stayed up late with me to rush projects, research problems, and talk about the future.

Sometimes when I look back at photos,

I realize that many people I once thought would naturally stay beside me have now walked toward different lives.

Some graduated.

Some went on to higher education.

Some started working.

Some changed direction.

Some slowly disappeared from this circle.

---

The old me used to think:

As long as everyone worked hard enough, we would keep walking on the same road.

Only later did I realize that life does not work that way.

Life is more like a very long journey.

Everyone has their own choices.

Everyone also has their own destination.

Some people will stay in the Cybersecurity community.

Some will move into AI.

Some will choose the medical field.

Some will begin studying robotics.

Some may even leave technology entirely.

None of these choices are right or wrong.

In the end, everyone simply walks toward a different direction.

---

I once thought I would always stay in the security community.

From learning, to competitions, to my future career.

I always thought I would continue along this path.

But later, I began touching robotics.

I began touching medical topics.

I began touching education and team leadership.

I found that what I truly liked might not be technology itself.

It was the process of using technology to help others, lead others, and complete something together.

So I began shifting from competitor to teaching assistant.

From teaching assistant to team leader.

From team leader, I slowly began learning how to become part of a team's staff and support structure.

This process was not easy.

But it let me see scenery I could not see before.

---

I began to understand the pressure teachers carry.

I began to understand the responsibility of leading a team.

I began to understand why some people are willing to spend so much time helping others.

Because when you see a student who originally understood nothing

slowly grow,

slowly improve,

and finally become able to stand on their own,

that sense of achievement is completely different from winning an award yourself.

---

So this blog is not only technical notes.

It is not a place for showing off achievements.

It is more like a memoir.

A memoir of my growth.

There will be success here.

There will also be failure.

There will be technology.

There will also be stories.

There will be competitions.

There will also be confusion and reflection from late nights.

---

Over these years,

I went from being a student who simply liked Cybersecurity

to becoming a teaching assistant,

then a team leader,

then someone who manages a team,

and I also began learning how to take care of a group of people who are curious about the future, just like I once was.

During this process,

I discovered that the truly precious things were never only technology.

Not awards.

Not rankings.

But the people who walked this road with me.

---

Some of them are still beside me.

Some have gone to different schools.

Some are preparing their own future.

Some are already working hard on a new road in life.

Maybe one day in the future,

we will no longer compete together.

No longer stay up late solving challenges together.

No longer rush projects together.

Maybe we will not even stay in contact.

But these memories will not disappear.

---

Maybe many years later,

I will no longer play CTF.

No longer lead teams.

No longer join competitions.

Maybe I will no longer be doing the things I am doing now.

But I hope that when my future self returns here,

I can still remember this journey.

Remember the people who fought beside me.

Remember the teachers who once changed me.

Remember the teammates who stayed up late with me to finish projects.

Remember the people who talked with me when I was low.

Remember the people who once believed in me.

---

Maybe one day in the future,

you will shine in different corners of the world.

Some will become engineers.

Some will become researchers.

Some will become teachers.

Some will start businesses.

Some will complete their own dreams.

And I do not know whether we will have the chance to meet again.

But at least here,

inside this small blog,

the traces of your existence will not disappear.

What is recorded here is not only technology.

It is also the youth of a group of people.

---

> To everyone who once walked with me.
>
> Maybe one day in the future, we will shine in different corners of the world.
>
> But at least here, your stories will not be forgotten.

And my blog will always remember you.
