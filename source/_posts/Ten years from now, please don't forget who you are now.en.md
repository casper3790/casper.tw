# Ten Years From Now, Please Don't Forget Who You Are Now

If you are reading this article now,

it means at least ten years have passed.

By then, maybe you are already an engineer.

Maybe you are doing research in graduate school.

Maybe you are working at some company.

Maybe you started a business.

Maybe you are living a life I cannot imagine right now.

But no matter where you are,

I hope you stop for a few minutes first.

Look back at who you were ten years ago.

---

## Who Am I Right Now?

Right now, I am a student.

A student who likes Cybersecurity, robotics, Web development, AI, and all kinds of technology fields.

Every day, there are always too many things I want to do.

I always feel like there is not enough time.

I always feel like there are still many things I do not know.

I also always feel like I am still far away from my dreams.

Sometimes I stay up late rushing projects.

Sometimes I practice for competitions until very late.

Sometimes I doubt myself because I failed.

And sometimes, one small bit of progress can make me happy for a long time.

---

## What Am I Doing Right Now?

Right now, I am learning many things.

I am playing CTF.

I am learning Cybersecurity.

I am studying Web development.

I am exploring Medical Cybersecurity.

I am learning ROS2.

I am researching autonomous mobile robots.

I am working hard to build a robot that can truly move.

I am also trying to lead a group of students who love technology as much as I do.

Sometimes it is exhausting.

But I really like this kind of life.

---

## Please Do Not Forget Those People

You, ten years from now.

I do not know whether you still remember the people who once stayed beside you.

The people who competed with you.

The people who stayed up late with you to rush projects.

The people who talked about the future with you.

The people who once believed in you.

The people who once helped you.

And the people you once helped.

Maybe everyone has already gone toward different lives.

Some may have become engineers.

Some may have become teachers.

Some may have started businesses.

Some may no longer touch technology at all.

But please do not forget.

They all once appeared in your youth.

---

## Please Do Not Forget Those Failures

If you are doing well ten years from now,

please do not forget that the person you are now once failed.

You once lost in competitions.

You once got rejected in applications.

You once wondered whether you were suited for this path.

You once felt sad for a long time because of an email, a result, or a competition.

But it is also because of those failures

that you slowly became who you are today.

---

## Please Do Not Forget Why You Started

The future you may already have many things.

Status.

Salary.

Reputation.

Titles.

Achievements.

But I hope you do not forget.

When you first started learning Cybersecurity,

started joining competitions,

and started studying technology,

it was not actually for those things.

It was because you truly found it interesting.

You truly liked it.

You truly wanted to know the answer.

---

## Please Also Remember the Qualities You Admire

Along the way,

I have rarely treated any one person as a true idol.

But some characters in stories have quietly influenced me for a long time.

Not because they are especially strong.

Not because they are perfect.

But because they carry qualities I have always admired.

### An Obsession With the Truth

There is a kind of person

who, no matter how complex a situation becomes,

does not easily accept the answers given by others.

They are always used to thinking for themselves.

Finding evidence themselves.

Piecing together what really happened by themselves.

Even when the truth is not beautiful.

Even when the result is not what they expected.

They are still willing to keep searching.

I hope the future you

does not lose this curiosity for the truth.

Do not stop thinking because of age, status, or experience.

And do not give up your own judgment because of other people's voices.

### Carrying Responsibility

There is also a kind of person

who quietly carries responsibility where others cannot see.

They are not necessarily the most dazzling people.

But when they are needed, they step forward.

They protect the people they cherish.

They protect the things they believe in.

Sometimes they even have to sacrifice their time, emotions, and ideals

just so the people around them can walk farther.

Over these years,

as I began leading teams,

helping junior students,

and trying to carry some responsibilities,

I slowly began to understand that feeling.

It turns out that growth is not only about making yourself stronger.

It is also about whether,

when someone needs you,

you are willing to become someone they can rely on.

### The Courage to Never Give Up

There is another kind of person

who keeps moving forward

even when they know the road ahead will be difficult,

even when they know the result may not be what they want.

They are not fearless.

They are people who are afraid, and still choose to stand up afterward.

Life is probably like this too.

No one is smooth forever.

No one can succeed all the time.

Everyone falls.

Everyone gets lost.

Everyone doubts themselves.

But what truly matters is never how many times you fall.

It is whether you are still willing to move forward after falling.

---

## If You Have Already Succeeded

If you, ten years from now, have already completed many goals,

please do not look down on who I am now.

Because the current me is trying desperately to become you.

I do not know what the future will look like.

But I know that the current me is really working hard.

So I hope the future you can also feel proud of who I am now.

---

## If You Have Not Succeeded Yet

If you, ten years from now, realize that you still have not achieved all your dreams,

that is okay too.

Because the current me has not done it either.

We are both still on the road.

And that was always part of life.

---

## Finally

Casper, ten years from now.

If one day you happen to return to this blog

and see this article,

I hope you can remember who you are now.

Remember those late nights.

Remember the people who worked hard with you.

Remember the moments when no one expected much from you.

Remember the version of yourself that once believed in dreams.

I hope that by then,

you still remain curious.

Still willing to learn.

Still willing to help others.

Still willing to believe in the future.

If you have done that,

then I think

the current me will be very happy.

---

> May the you of ten years from now still remember who you are now.
>
> -- Casper, 2026
